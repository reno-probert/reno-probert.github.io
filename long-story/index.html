Deepest Diving Aquatic Mammals

Aquatic mammals all need to surface in order to breath air, but some mammals are able to dive to incredible depths. Scroll down to see examples of some of the deepest diving marine mammals and deepest known depths of the ocean they can reach.

900 meters - Weddel Seal

Weddel seals are a relatively common species of seal that are able to hold their breath for more than 45 minutes when diving. Research has shown that weddel seals strategically plan their dives based on light for visual hunting.

1,500 meters - Narwhal

Narwhals perform frequent deep dives for hunting purposes but mostly in the winter months. Scientists have struggled to determine the exact reason for the narwhal's diving patterns but due to their arctic habitat, it is theorized that the pattern has to do with avoiding ice on the ocean surface.

2,000 meters - Sperm Whale

Sperm whales are able to reach incredible depths and are the largest deep diving mammal on this list. The whales have massive oxygen reserves and are able to deep dive and hunt large prey including giant squid.

2,338 meters - Elephant Seal

Elephant seals are the deepest diving mammal that is not a whale. Elephant seals have specialized cardiovascular systems that slow their heartrate and maintain oxygen levels in the blood allowing them to keep up oxygen levels while emptying their lungs to prevent depth related complications.

2,992 meters - Beaked Whale

The Cuvier's beaked whale holds the record for the deepest dive of any marine animal and can hold it's breath for over 3 hours. The whale has collapsable lungs that make it possible to deal with the crushing weight of deep water. There are multiple deep-diving beaked whales but the Cuvier's is the champion diver by hundreds of meters.
