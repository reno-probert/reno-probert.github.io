Deepest Diving Air Breathers in the Animal Kingdom

Aquatic mammals and birds all need to breath air, but some mammals are able to dive to incredible depths and hold their breath for impressive lengths of time. Scroll down to see examples of some of the deepest diving marine mammals and birds and the deepest known depths of the ocean they can reach.

100 meters - Sea Otter

Sea Otters are the deepest diving mammals that are in the same family as seals or whales. Sea otters regularly dive down to the ocean floor in order to hunt for shellfish.

210 meters - Thick-Billed Murre

Thick-Billed Murre are the deepest diving bird that is not in the penguin family. Murre use their wings to propell through the water and are able to store oxygen in their muscles and blood rather than their lungs which reduces buoyancy.

500 meters - Emperor Penguins

Emperor penguins are the largest species of penguins and also the deepest divers of any bird species. At depths of 500 meters emperor penguins can hunt at depths greater than the range of operation for most submarines.

900 meters - Weddel Seal

Weddel seals are a relatively common species of seal that are able to hold their breath for more than 45 minutes when diving. Research has shown that weddel seals strategically plan their dives based on light for visual hunting.

1,500 meters - Narwhal

Narwhals perform frequent deep dives for hunting purposes but mostly in the winter months. Scientists have struggled to determine the exact reason for the narwhal's diving patterns but due to their arctic habitat, it is theorized that the pattern has to do with avoiding ice on the ocean surface.

2,000 meters - Sperm Whale

Sperm whales are able to reach incredible depths and are the largest deep diving mammal on this list. The whales have massive oxygen reserves and are able to deep dive and hunt large, deep water prey including giant squid.

2,338 meters - Elephant Seal

Elephant seals are the deepest diving mammal that is not a whale. Elephant seals have specialized cardiovascular systems that slow their heartrate and maintain oxygen levels in the blood allowing them to keep up oxygen levels while emptying their lungs to prevent depth related complications. While sperm whales are more consistantly seen diving to depths of around 2,000 feet, the southern elephant seal has been recorded as diving deeper than any known sperm whale by reaching the 2,338 meter mark.

2,992 meters - Beaked Whale

The Cuvier's beaked whale holds the record for the deepest dive of any marine animal and can hold it's breath for over 3 hours. The whale has collapsable lungs that make it possible to deal with the crushing weight of deep water. There are multiple deep-diving beaked whales to the point where half of this list could be beaked whales of some variety, but the Cuvier's is the champion diver by hundreds of meters.
